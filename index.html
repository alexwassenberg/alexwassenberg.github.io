about me

In May 2016, I graduated from the University of Colorado Boulder with my MS in Aerospace Engineering. I've always been fascinated by human spaceflight and exploration, and so I spent a lot of my time at CU working in the Bioastronautics Lab. BioAstro is the study of how we keep humans alive and productive in space through the design of space habitats, life support systems, and payloads so that we can send astronauts farther out into space for longer periods of time.

I completed my BS in Aerospace Engineering with a minor in Architecture at MIT in June 2014.

Below is a selection of projects and research I have been involved in.

academic projects

Autonomous Martian Greenhouse Prototype

Designed and constructed reduced-scale prototype Martian greenhouse to autonomously monitor and control plant growth through the regulation of lighting, nutrient and water provision, and atmospheric composition.

Responsibilities:
• atmosphere regulation system
• structure
• electronics integration

Cygnus Module Space Habitat Mock Up

Designed and constructed full-scale mock up of the Orbital ATK Cygnus module (currently used to carry cargo to the ISS) with a reconfigurable internal structure for human factors and subsystem layout testing in order to develop an interior layout suitable for human habitation.

Responsibilities:
• Mock up external shell design and construction

Spacecraft Inspection Satellite Imaging Sensor Integration

Carried out software and hardware integration for a suite of three imaging sensors onto a microsatellite platform to be used for on-orbit spacecraft inspection.

Responsibilities:
• thermal imaging camera testing and integration subsystem lead
• thermal imaging camera software

research experience

Long-Duration Habitat Medical Unit

Designed the interior volume and layout of a space habitat medical unit to accommodate nominal through advanced life support medical care of astronauts on long-duration space missions.

Silica Gel Regeneration Cycling for Habitat Humidity Control

Led testing to characterize humidity removal performance of a regenerable silica gel dessicant bed to determine the optimal desorption temperature for implementation in spacecraft environmental control systems.

Electrode Development for Ion Electrospray Propulsion Systems

Used a novel materials development process (known as the "sol-gel" process) to grow highly uniform and tailorable electrodes for ion electrospray propulsion systems and which produce current outputs two orders of magnitude greater than those made from leading materials with reduced production time and cost.
