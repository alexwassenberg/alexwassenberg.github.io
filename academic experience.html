academic projects

Autonomous Martian Greenhouse Prototype

Purpose: Worked in a team of 14 students to design and construct a reduced-scale prototype Martian greenhouse sponsored by the NASA eXploration Habitat Academic Innovation Challenge. The greenhouse was designed to be closed system that would autonomously monitor and control plant growth through the regulation of lighting, nutrient and water provision, and atmospheric composition.

Role: My responsibilities included work on the atmosphere regulation, structural, and electrical design teams. As a member of the atmosphere regulation subsystem, I worked on the design and construction of the systems required to carry out carbon dioxide provision for plant growth, nitrogen provision to maintain to pressure in the growing volume, removal and separation of plant-produced oxygen, humidity control, trace contaminent control, and ventilation. This work required the definition of subsystem requirements; development of mass, power, volume, and cost budgets; writing and implementation of component and system integration test plans; communication with vendors to identify and modify COTS components suitable for use in the system; hardware integration; and the development of interface control documentation to specify the integration of the atmosphere regulation subsystem components with other subsystem components and the system structure.

As a member of the structural design subsystem, I worked on the design (in SolidWorks) and construction of an 80/20 framework that would enable the integration of all subsystem componenets and also provide a sealed growing volume to enable an isolated, customized atmosphere.

My work on the electrical subsystem included the assembly of the support circuitry for the system's 35 sensors and wiring of the system. Additionally, I modeled the system PCB in Altium, completed PCB assembly, and integrated the PCB into the system.
